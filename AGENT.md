# Agent Instructions: Diner-Style Food Website

## Objective

Build interfaces inspired by the Denny's website: bold, food-first, diner-friendly, and optimized for ordering. The experience should feel energetic, practical, and easy to scan.

## Core Rules

- Prioritize food imagery, ordering actions, rewards, and location discovery.
- Use strong contrast with red, yellow, black, white, and warm neutral backgrounds.
- Keep the first screen useful: show food, navigation, and a clear primary CTA.
- Do not create a generic marketing landing page unless specifically requested.
- Do not copy Denny's logos, proprietary images, exact campaign artwork, character partnerships, or protected copy.
- Use original or licensed assets and original campaign language.

## Layout Guidance

- Use full-width sections with constrained inner content.
- Do not place cards inside other cards.
- Keep product cards stable in height and easy to compare.
- Use carousels only when they include visible controls and keyboard-accessible navigation.
- Make mobile ordering paths obvious and reachable.
- Keep the header compact, with order and location actions easy to find.

## Visual Guidance

- Use bright, appetizing food photography.
- Use large, bold, condensed headings for hero and promotional content.
- Use practical body copy and short CTA labels.
- Use `8px` or smaller card radius unless the existing project design system says otherwise.
- Use familiar icons for menu, cart, profile, add, close, previous, next, and location.
- Use yellow/red badges for deals, new items, and limited-time offers.

## Interaction Guidance

- Buttons need visible hover, active, disabled, and focus states.
- Navigation drawers and dropdowns must close predictably.
- Ordering controls should never be hidden behind unclear gestures.
- Respect reduced-motion settings.
- Avoid slow decorative animation that delays the ordering flow.

## Accessibility Requirements

- Use semantic landmarks: header, nav, main, section, footer.
- Provide accessible labels for icon-only controls.
- Give informative images useful alt text.
- Ensure text contrast meets WCAG AA.
- Ensure keyboard users can operate menus, carousels, modals, and order controls.
- Keep tap targets at least `44px`.

## Content Rules

- Write in a warm, direct, appetite-driven voice.
- Prefer short CTAs: "Order Now", "Learn More", "Join Rewards", "Find a Location".
- Keep promo copy brief and concrete.
- Do not use long explanatory text inside the app UI.
- Avoid luxury, minimalist, or overly formal restaurant language.

## Development Checklist

Before finishing:

- Confirm the first viewport clearly communicates the food or offer.
- Confirm the primary ordering CTA is visible on desktop and mobile.
- Confirm product images are not distorted or overly cropped.
- Confirm text does not overlap images, buttons, or controls.
- Confirm keyboard focus is visible.
- Confirm responsive layouts work at mobile, tablet, and desktop widths.
